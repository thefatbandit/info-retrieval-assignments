Let's now turn to the current status of our InnovAATe Phase 3 clinical program for our proprietary Inhaled AAT for the treatment of Alpha-1 Antitrypsin deficiency. As you will recall, we've been recruiting patients into the Phase 3 trial in Europe through February 2020. However, as we said in our last call in March, due to the effect of the COVID-19 pandemic on healthcare systems, recruitment into the study was temporarily halted. I am pleased to report today that recruitment into the study restarted in June.

As a reminder, InnovAATe is a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled pivotal Phase 3 trial designed to assess the efficacy and safety of Inhaled AAT in patients with Alpha-1 deficiency and moderate lung disease. Up to 250 patients will be randomized one-to-one to receive either Inhaled AAT at the dose of 80 milligrams once daily or placebo of two years of treatment. The primary endpoint of the InnovAATe trial is lung function measured by FEV1. Secondary endpoints include changes in lung density as measured by CT scan, as well as other parameters of disease severity such as additional pulmonary functions, exacerbation rate, and six-minute walk test.
