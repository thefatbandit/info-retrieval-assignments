Margaret Wigglesworth

Thanks, Katie. I'd like to begin with an update on the COVID-19 environment. As of August 1, approximately 4.6% of our resident population has tested positive for COVID-19. Of these confirmed resident cases, roughly 43% of the residents have since recovered from the virus as defined by the CDC guidelines.

Generally, we have seen concentrations of cases similar to those which the CDC has highlighted as areas significantly impacted by the virus. As of August 1, 7 states represent more than half of our cases and include: Florida, New Jersey, Maryland, South Carolina, Illinois, Georgia and Texas.

Before I review the senior living operating results, I'd like to take a moment to walk through our approach to reopening. Five Star has implemented a phased approach to reopening communities, utilizing 3 levels that slowly ease restrictions when appropriate. The levels of reopening are designed to ease internal restrictions first, then certain external restrictions and finally lead into a new normal operating environment.

Level 1 allows communities to slowly ease internal restrictions, allowing residents limited movement around the community with appropriate reconfiguration of physical spaces, more regulated activity and use of certain amenities on a limited and highly prescheduled bases. We've developed programming that served to educate team members and residents on the importance of continuing to wear masks, social distance, properly disinfect and limit the size of gathering. Family business may also resume on a limited basis with restrictions in outdoor areas.

At Level 2, communities may ease some external restrictions, such as allowing certain nonessential visitors into the communities on a limited basis, subject to screening and continuing to reinforce the behaviors related to infection control.

Level 3 focuses on the stable, long-term approach to the new normal, which includes a resumption of most activities, but in a way that incorporates all of the new behaviors in levels 1 and 2. Level 3 also requires the continuation of the screening, infection control, testing and contact tracing measures that have been put into place to mitigate the risk of infection.

To be sure the surveillance retesting process is an important component of the Five Star reopening criteria as well as the impact of the virus on the local area within which our community is located, we use this information and other gating criteria to determine eligibility for our community to move to the next level. We have implemented the planning and approval process that each community must meet so that a consistent and measured approach is executed across all of Five Star's portfolio, ensuring that each community receives the support and resources required to successfully evolve as COVID-19 continues to present challenges.

These guidelines have been tailored to the specific needs of each line of business, but allows flexibility within each level of reopening so that communities can tailor their reopening plans to local needs and preferences.

During the second quarter of 2020, occupancy and rate across our senior living communities declined due to the effects of COVID-19. Average occupancy in our comparable community owned and leased portfolio dropped 300 basis points from both the sequential and prior year quarter. While comparable community average occupancy in our managed communities decreased 600 basis points year-over-year and roughly 380 basis points from the last quarter's results.

Our revenue management team analyzes pricing on a case-by-case basis to optimize rate. We are seeing rate pressure as a result of the pandemic. The competitors are offering concessions in areas more impacted by COVID-19 to attract new residents where movements are feasible. As a result of the current environment, comparable community RevPAR for the owned and leased portfolio was down 6% from the same period last year. While comparable community RevPAR in our managed portfolio was down 5.7%.

While the pandemic has significantly impacted our ability to pursue leads and convert move-ins, there is evidence of pent-up demand. Given that community move-ins are restricted by active confirmed cases of COVID-19, we began tracking many of our delayed sales leads and maintained constant contact with these prospective residents. We are evaluating a process to thoughtfully facilitate move-ins where the risk is low and all constituents in the process remain safe. While we are optimistic about slowing the occupancy declines through these measures, as the pandemic runs its course, we continue to expect deterioration in our occupancy in the near term.
